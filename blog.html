Graduating Bootcamp

graduating insanity
10.31.2017

I figured I earned a few days break from living and breathing code after graduation. Coming back to it after a long weekend was when I truly realized how challenging it would be to retain the knowledge I learned!

Its an amazing privilege to be immersed in such a rich environment for 8-10 hours a day with equally enthusiastic peers. But its a real concern that the information attained in such an intensive setting can slip away just as easily. On top of that, now you're left on your own. Its a real challenge, so my mission now is to revisit and solidify the frameworks and technologies we covered during the course.

Project 4: Student Thesis

mitigating a socket disaster
10.27.2017

The most difficult challenge of this project was understanding the complex data flow when introducing web-sockets to a React App (which already has a pretty confusing data structure). My initial plan was to make a chat room application for my thesis. But after a few days, I still had nothing and was extremely confused as to how my entire backend should function. I just couldn’t get the user functionality to work...

I was just about to ditch the whole idea, when I decided to forget users for a minute to play around with simple add/sub buttons with sockets. It worked! It made sense why it was working! And that was the spark that led me to just make a game instead. And in the end, focusing on 2 users helped me get a better grasp on web-sockets than doing a larger chat app.
